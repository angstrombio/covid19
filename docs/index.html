About the Map

SARS-CoV-2 possesses a combination of infectiousness and virulence that has overwhelmed unprepared healthcare systems worldwide. Reports from Wuhan, China, Iran, Lombardy, Italy, and now from New York City and other regions of the United States indicate that an inability to control the spread of the disease in the early weeks of a localized outbreak can lead to a flood of patients who require intensive care for acute respiratory distress or otherwise life-threatening symptoms. This flood can overwhelm local and regional healthcare system capacity with frightening speed, and can send mortality rates soaring.

We have been inspired and humbled by the response to the outbreak, and we produced this resource to address both our own needs for COVID-19 planning, and also in the hope that it can help as many people as possible contextualize, plan, and respond to COVID-19.

We would love any feedback you may have - be it questions, comments, requests for analysis, or even complaints. Whatever the context or content, please do not hesitate to reach out to us. And in the meantime, we hope you and yours remain as unscathed as possible by this outbreak, and that life returns to a healthy normal, soon.

The Angstrom Bio Team

Data on the map is presented by census statistical regions (Metropolitan Statistical Area or Micropolital Statistical Area) (MSA), as defined below. We have aggregated county-level data from within these regions as we believe it best represents the true picture and risk associated with each region. LAYERS

The map presents color-coded data in a number of layers, controlled via the selector at the upper right. Currently, we are presenting:

  • Cases per ICU Bed: The total number of cases in a region divided by the Number of ICU Beds in that region (defined below).
  • Cases per 10,000 People: The total number of cases in a region compared to the 2018 U.S. Census population estimates for that region.
  • New Cases: The number of new cases in a region from the latest daily update to this data.
  • Deaths: Total number of deaths from COVID-19 as reported by local and state departments of health.
  • Population: The 2018 census estimates of population for a region.
  • Cases: Cumulative number of cases in a given region

Additional information on each region is displayed when clicking on or moving over a region.

DEFINITIONS

In addition to the data layers defined above, the following terms are used throughout our analysis and data presentation:

  • Metropolitan Statistical Area: One of 542 statistical regions in the United States generally defined as having more than 50,000 residents.
  • Micropolital Statistical Area: One of 384 statistical regions in the United States generally defined as having more than 10,000 residents but less than 50,000 residents.
  • Number of Hospitals: Total number of healthcare institutions in a given region that provide inpatient, therapeutic, or rehabilitation services, under the supervision of physicians.
  • Number of Hospital Beds: The sum of the hospital-reported "adult, pediatric, birthing room, or newborn ICU beds (excluding newborn bassinets) maintained in a patient care area for lodging patients in acute, long term, or domiciliary areas of a given hospital" in a given region.
  • Number of ICU Beds: The sum of the beds in a given region that fit the definition set forth in CMS, Section 2202.7, 22-8.2.
